The History of Prime Numbers: The Unsolved Puzzle

Umay Gokturk December 2025 History of Mathematics
Abstract: This paper explores the history of prime numbers from ancient civilizations to modern applications. Beginning with early artifacts like the Ishango Bone and Babylonian clay tablets, it traces the development of prime number theory through Ancient Greek mathematicians Euclid and Eratosthenes, Islamic scholars including Thabit Ibn-Qurra and Ibn al-Haytham, and finally to modern European mathematicians. The paper focuses on key contributions from Mersenne, Fermat, Euler, Gauss, and Riemann, culminating in the famous Riemann Hypothesisâ€”one of the most important unsolved problems in mathematics. The paper demonstrates how a simple question about prime numbers has united mathematicians across thousands of years.
